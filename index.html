FAQ's

An ENT doctor, or otolaryngologist, treats conditions related to the ear, nose, and throat. Common conditions include ear infections, sinus problems, sleep apnea, snoring, throat pain, and hearing loss.

Pediatric ENT specialists are trained to diagnose and treat ear, nose, and throat conditions specifically in children, considering their unique medical needs and growth patterns. Adult ENTs focus on treating ear, nose, and throat issues in adults.

If you're experiencing persistent or recurring symptoms like ear pain, sinus problems, sore throat, difficulty swallowing, or hearing loss, it's time to see an ENT specialist. They can offer advanced treatments and ensure you get the best care for your condition.
